# 《自深深处》阅读记录

**日期**：2026-04-17  
**范围**：第7-16句原文

> If you find one false excuse for yourself, you will soon find a hundred, and be just what you were before.  
> Do you still say, as you said to Robbie in your answer, that I "attribute unworthy motives" to you?  
> Ah! you have no motives in life. You had appetites merely.  
> A motive is an intellectual aim.  
> That you were "very young" when our friendship began?  
> Your defect was not that you knew so little about life, but that you knew so much.  
> The morning dawn of boyhood with its delicate bloom, its clear pure light, its joy of innocence and expectation you had left far behind.  
> With very swift and running feet you had passed from Romance to Realism.  
> The gutter and the things that live in it had begun to fascinate you.  
> That was the origin of the trouble in which you sought my aid, and I, so unwisely according to the wisdom of this world, out of pity and kindness gave it to you.

---

## 翻译
如果你为自己找到一个借口，那你很快就会找到一百个，然后变回你以前的样子。  你还在说——像你在给罗比的回信里说的那样——我把“卑劣的动机归咎于”你吗？  啊！你的人生才没有动机。你只有欲望而已。  动机是理性的目标。  
我们友谊开始时你还“过于年少”？  你的问题不是对人生知道得太少，而是知道得太多。  少年的起始，伴随着无风自摇的盛放、干净纯粹的光辉、天真无邪和满怀憧憬的欢欣，而你，早已把这些远远抛之脑后了。  你用极其迅捷的脚步从浪漫主义跑向了现实主义。  阴沟和里面的东西早就在蛊惑你。  这就是你来像我寻求帮助时所要解决的麻烦的根源，而我——按照这个世界的评判标准来看，是多么地不明智——出于怜悯和善意，给了你帮助。

## 结构拆解

### 第1句
> If you find one false excuse for yourself, you will soon find a hundred, and be just what you were before.
>
> [条件状语从句] If you find one false excuse for yourself
> │
> └─ [主句] you will soon find a hundred, and be just what you were before
> ├─ 并列谓语1: will find a hundred
> └─ 并列谓语2: (will) be just what you were before
> └─ [表语从句] what you were before

### 第2-4句（one false excuse）

> Do you still say, as you said to Robbie in your answer, that I "attribute unworthy motives" to you? Ah! you have no motives in life. You had appetites merely. A motive is an intellectual aim.
>
> [问句] Do you still say ... that I "attribute unworthy motives" to you?
> ├─ [插入语] as you said to Robbie in your answer
> └─ [宾语从句] that I "attribute unworthy motives" to you
>
> [陈述1] Ah! you have no motives in life.
> [陈述2] You had appetites merely.
> [陈述3] A motive is an intellectual aim.第5-10句

### 第5-10句（another false excuse）

> That you were "very young" when our friendship began?  Your defect was not that you knew so little about life, but that you knew so much.  The morning dawn of boyhood with its delicate bloom, its clear pure light, its joy of innocence and expectation you had left far behind.  With very swift and running feet you had passed from Romance to Realism.  
> The gutter and the things that live in it had begun to fascinate you.  That was the origin of the trouble in which you sought my aid, and I, so unwisely according to the wisdom of this world, out of pity and kindness gave it to you.
>
> 5. That you were "very young" when our friendship began?
>
> [反问/省略] (Do you say) That you were "very young" when our friendship began?
> └─ [时间状语从句] when our friendship began
>
> 6. Your defect was not that you knew so little about life, but that you knew so much.  
>
> [主句] Your defect was not A but B
> ├─ A: that you knew so little about life
> └─ B: that you knew so much
>
> 7. The morning dawn of boyhood with its delicate bloom, its clear pure light, its joy of innocence and expectation you had left far behind.
>
> [倒装] The morning dawn of boyhood (with ...) you had left far behind.
> └─ 正常语序：You had left the morning dawn of boyhood (with ...) far behind.
>
> 8. With very swift and running feet you had passed from Romance to Realism.
>
> [状语] With very swift and running feet
> └─ [主句] you had passed from Romance to Realism
>
> 9. The gutter and the things that live in it had begun to fascinate you. 
>
>  [主句] The gutter and the things had begun to fascinate you.
> └─ [定语从句] that live in it (修饰 the things)
>
> 10. That was the origin of the trouble in which you sought my aid, and I, so unwisely according to the wisdom of this world, out of pity and kindness gave it to you.
>
> [主句1] That was the origin of the trouble
> └─ [定语从句] in which you sought my aid (修饰 trouble)
>
> [并列主句2] and I, so unwisely according to the wisdom of this world, out of pity and kindness gave it to you.
> ├─ 插入语: so unwisely according to the wisdom of this world
> └─ 状语: out of pity and kindness
