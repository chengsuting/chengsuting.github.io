# 《自深深处》阅读记录

**日期**：2026-05-03  
**范围**：第五段整段

---

## 原文

> I remember, for instance, in September '93, to select merely one instance out of many, taking a set of chambers, purely in order to work undisturbed, as I had broken my contract with John Hare from whom I had promised to write a play, and who was pressing me on the subject. 
>
> During the first week you kept away. We had, not unnaturally indeed, differed on the question of the artistic value of your translation of *Salome*, so you contented yourself with sending me foolish letters on the subject. In that week I wrote and completed in every detail, as it was ultimately performed, the first act of *An Ideal Husband*. 
>
> The second week you returned and my work practically had to be given up.
>
>  I arrived at St James's place every morning at 11.30, in order to have the opportunity of thinking and writing without the interruptions inseparable from my own household, quiet and peaceful as that household was. But the attempt was vain. At twelve o'clock you drove up, and stayed smoking cigarettes and chattering till 1.30, when I had to take you out to luncheon at the Café Royal or the Berkeley. Luncheon with its liqueurs lasted usually till 3.30. For an hour you retired to White's. At tea-time you appeared again, and stayed till it was time to dress for dinner. You dined with me either at the Savoy or at Tite Street. We did not separate as a rule till after midnight, as supper at Willis's had to wind up the entrancing day. 
>
> That was my life for those three months, every single day, except during the four days when you went abroad. I then, of course, had to go over to Calais to fetch you back. 
>
> For one of my nature and temperament it was a position at once grotesque and tragic.

## 翻译

我记得，例如在1893年9月——仅从众多例子中挑一个——我租了一套房间，纯粹为了不受打扰地工作，因为我已经违反了我与约翰·黑尔签订的合同，我曾答应为他写一部戏，他一直就此事在催我。

第一周，你没有出现。我们在——这很自然——你翻译的《莎乐美》的艺术价值问题上产生了分歧，于是你退而求其次就这件事给我写些愚蠢的信。那一周，我写出了《理想丈夫》的第一幕，并在每一个细节上都把它完成了，正如它最终上演时那样。

第二周你回来了，我几乎不得不放弃我的工作。

我每天早上11点半赶到 St James's place，为的是有机会思考和写作，避开我家里不可避免的打扰——尽管那个家安静平和。但这一尝试是徒劳的。12点钟，你坐着马车来了，然后一直抽烟、闲聊到1点半，这时我不得不带你去Café Royal或Berkeley吃午餐。那顿配有利口酒的午餐通常要吃到3点半。有一个小时的时间你抽身去了White's俱乐部。到下午茶时间，再次出现，一直待到该换衣服去晚餐的时候。你要么在Savoy要么在Tite Street和我一起用晚餐。通常我们直到午夜过后才分开，因为还要去Willis’s吃宵夜，以结束这迷人的一天。

这就是我三个月里的生活，每一天都是如此，除了你出国的那四天。那四天里，当然，我还必须去加莱把你接回来。

对于我这样天性和气质的人来说，真是一种既荒诞又可悲的处境。
