# 《自深深处》阅读记录

**日期**：2026-04-21  
**范围**：第25-30句

---

## 原文
> Remember also that whatever is misery to you to read, is still greater misery to me to set down.  
> To you the Unseen Powers have been very good.  
> They have permitted you to see the strange and tragic shapes of Life as one sees shadows in a crystal.  
> The head of Medusa that turns living men to stone, you have been allowed to look at in a mirror merely.  
> You yourself have walked free among the flowers.  
> From me the beautiful world of colour and motion has been taken away.

---

## 翻译
同样记住：任何你读来觉得痛苦的事情，我在写它的时候更加痛苦。  
对你而言，无形的力量一直非常仁慈。  
它们允许你看见生命奇异而悲惨的形态，就像一个人观看水晶中的影子。  
美杜莎那能把活人变成石头的头颅，你也仅仅被允许通过镜子观看。  
你自己一直在花丛中自由游走。  
于我，那个多姿多彩的美丽世界已经被夺走了。

## 结构分析

### 第一句

[主句] Remember
└─ [宾语从句] that whatever is misery to you to read is still greater misery to me to set down
├─ 主语从句：whatever is misery to you to read
│ ├─ whatever (主语)
│ ├─ is (系动词)
│ ├─ misery (表语)
│ └─ to you / to read (状语/定语)
└─ 主句谓语：is still greater misery to me to set down

### 第二句

[状语] To you
└─ [主句] the Unseen Powers have been very good

└─ 定语从句：that turns living men to stone
